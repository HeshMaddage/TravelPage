Rome, Italy

Rome, the capital of Italy, is a city rich in history, culture, and architectural splendor, earning its nickname "The Eternal City." With iconic ancient landmarks like the Colosseum, the Roman Forum, and the Pantheon, Rome offers a fascinating glimpse into the grandeur of the Roman Empire. Enclosed within its borders is Vatican City, the spiritual heart of the Roman Catholic Church, home to St. Peter's Basilica and the Sistine Chapel. The city's Renaissance and Baroque architecture is exemplified by masterpieces such as the Trevi Fountain and St. Peter's Square. Rome's vibrant culinary scene features classic dishes like pasta carbonara and gelato, enjoyed in bustling markets and traditional trattorias. Cultural richness abounds with world-renowned museums, lively piazzas, and a thriving nightlife, particularly in neighborhoods like Trastevere. With its blend of ancient history and dynamic modern life, Rome remains a captivating destination for travelers from around the globe.
